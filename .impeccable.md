## Design Context

### Users
General Notion users — not developers or ops teams. People who have accumulated duplicates over time and want a reliable, easy-to-trust tool to clean them up. They may be anxious about data deletion. They need clarity and confidence, not complexity.

### Brand Personality
Bold, confident, modern. The interface should feel like a well-made, premium utility — something you'd recommend to someone. It should project competence without being cold or technical.

### Aesthetic Direction
Minimal + refined. Keep the current restraint but fix the generic AI-slop tells: improve typography (ditch system-ui defaults), sharpen visual hierarchy, add intentional rhythm to spacing. Should feel designed, not scaffolded.

**Anti-references:**
- Not Notion itself — this is a tool *about* Notion, not made by Notion. Avoid Notion's blocky editorial aesthetic and sans-serif neutrality.
- Not the AI purple app — no dark mode + glowing purple + glassmorphism combo.

### Design Principles
1. **Confidence through clarity** — users are about to delete data. The UI must project certainty. No ambiguous states, no wishy-washy labels.
2. **Restraint with intention** — every element earns its place. Whitespace is used deliberately, not leftover.
3. **Typography does the heavy lifting** — a strong, distinctive typeface replaces decoration. The font is the brand.
4. **Bold = purposeful, not loud** — boldness comes from decisive hierarchy and composition, not color loudness or visual noise.
5. **Never look like you came from a template** — distinctive enough that someone asks "who made this?" not "which AI generated this?"
